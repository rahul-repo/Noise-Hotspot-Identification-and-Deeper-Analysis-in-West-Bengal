# Noise Hotspot Identification in West Bengal

## Overview

This project investigates spatial and temporal patterns of environmental noise pollution in West Bengal. The primary objective is to identify noise hotspots and study regional variation in noise levels using modern statistical and spatial analytical techniques.

The study combines exploratory data analysis, clustering methods, Functional Principal Component Analysis (FPCA), and spatial statistics to understand the structure and dynamics of environmental noise across locations.

---

## Objectives

* Identify environmental noise hotspots in West Bengal.
* Analyze temporal variation in noise levels.
* Explore similarities and differences among monitoring locations.
* Detect spatial clustering and hotspot structures.
* Apply modern statistical techniques for environmental data analysis.

---

## Data

The analysis is based on environmental noise measurements collected from monitoring stations across West Bengal.

The dataset contains information on noise intensity across locations and time periods, enabling both spatial and temporal analysis.

---

## Methodology

The following statistical methods were employed:

### Exploratory Data Analysis

* Summary statistics
* Data visualization
* Pattern exploration

### Clustering Analysis

* Identification of groups of locations with similar noise characteristics.
* Detection of common spatial structures.

### Functional Principal Component Analysis (FPCA)

* Analysis of temporal noise variation.
* Dimension reduction for functional observations.
* Extraction of dominant temporal patterns.

### Spatial Statistics

* Moran's I for spatial autocorrelation analysis.
* Getis-Ord statistics for hotspot identification.
* Spatial pattern assessment and regional comparison.

## Results

The analysis identified regions exhibiting elevated environmental noise levels and revealed significant spatial and temporal variation in noise behaviour.

Hotspot detection methods and spatial autocorrelation measures provided insight into the geographic concentration of noise pollution across West Bengal.
